Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its simplicity and rich flavors. Made with spaghetti, eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta, and black pepper, this creamy delight offers a perfect balance of textures and tastes. The silky sauce clings to the pasta, creating a satisfying and indulgent experience with every bite, making it a timeless favorite in Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces (340g) spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 4 ounces (115g) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instruction

  1. Cook the Spaghetti:
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    • Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.
  2. Prepare the Sauce:
    • In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.
  3. Cook the Pancetta (or Guanciale) and Garlic:
    • In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the diced pancetta or guanciale until it becomes crispy and browned.
    • If using garlic, add it to the pan and saut√© for about a minute until fragrant.
  4. Combine Pasta and Sauce:
    • Add the cooked and drained spaghetti directly into the skillet with pancetta (or guanciale).
    • Toss to combine and coat the pasta with the pancetta and garlic.
  5. Add the Egg Mixture:
    • Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing vigorously to create a creamy sauce. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs, creating a silky sauce.
    • If the sauce is too thick, gradually add reserved pasta water until you achieve the desired consistency.
  6. Serve and Garnish:
    • Season with salt and more freshly ground black pepper to taste.
    • Garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Spaghetti Carbonara!!

Note: Traditional Carbonara recipes do not include cream, so the creamy texture comes solely from the eggs and cheese. Make sure to work quickly when combining the sauce with the hot pasta to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
